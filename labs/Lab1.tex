\section{Lab 1}\label{lab-1}

Spring 2025

\textbf{Copy and paste these questions into a document (Google Doc or
Word) to answer these questions.} Please number your responses to help
your GSI grade. There's no word count, and we won't penalize students
for grammar or spelling. Do your best to explain your ideas in your own
words, and if you get stuck or confused, post on Discord / ask your GSI
/ explain why you got stuck and what you tried to do to figure things
out.~

\begin{enumerate}
\def\labelenumi{\arabic{enumi}.}
\item
  \textbf{In Lecture / Chapter 2.} Define two variables in R - one
  numeric and one string variable (these can be unrelated to your
  project topic!) Make sure to collect at least ten data points for each
  variable, and show that you successfully defined the variable in R by
  ``printing'' it in R. Yeah, you're programming!
\item
  \textbf{In Lecture.} What's a prediction about people that you made
  today? What information did you use to make this prediction? How did
  (or could) you use this prediction to influence outcomes? Were you
  valid in your predictions?
\item
  \textbf{In Lecture / Section / On Your Own.} Get started on the final
  project by thinking through a research question you might be
  interested in studying as a psychology researcher. (Totally fine to
  change this, but great to start focusing on a question.)

  \begin{itemize}
  \tightlist
  \item
    What is your question? Why do you care about this question (and / or
    why does this question matter to others)? How interested in this
    question are you on a scale from 0 (just doing to get credit for
    this question) to 10 (this is what motivates you to wake up each day
    and you will answer this question with the energy and passion of
    1000 suns)?
  \item
    How do your past experiences and background inform this question?
  \item
    What is the variable that is the focus of this question? How does
    this variable relate to affect, behvaior, and cognition? Which
    aspect of this variable are you most interested in focusing on for
    your project?
  \item
    What is the between-person form of variation for this variable? What
    is the within-person form of variation for this variable?
    \textbf{Note : for the final project, I strongly recommend focusing
    on a between-person variation version of the variable for the final
    project.}
  \end{itemize}
\item
  \textbf{In Discussion Section.} With your discussion section,define
  each of the six biases described in the Goldacre (2010) reading on
  cognitive biases, and come up with an example from real-life.
\end{enumerate}
